Selected projects (clickable sections for future expansion)

Shield vs. Stratovolcanoes: Comparing Hazards of Volcanism in Hawaii and Washington

Course: GIS4324 GIS Analysis of Hazard Vulnerability Date: Spring 2024
  • Used ArcGIS Pro to compare and manipulate published hazard maps and supporting datasets (hazards, zoning, demographic layers).
  • Georeferenced a post-eruption hazard map and overlaid fatality locations to evaluate exposure relative to blast/lahar extents.
  • Conducted an exposure assessment by intersecting hazard footprints with census tracts and comparing risk context using FEMA’s National Risk Index (NRI).

Enhancing Mineral Exploration through Remote Sensing and Machine Learning: A Focus on Cobalt and Nickel

Course: GIS4037 Remote Sensing Date: Fall 2024
  • Applied remote-sensing methods to mineral exploration using Sentinel-2 spectral data and a DEM, including development/use of spectral-index approaches.
  • Implemented supervised machine learning (Random Tree / RF-style classification) with manually selected training points and known mining locations as training data for the Co/Ni class.
  • Combined spectral-index and machine-learning outputs into an integrated map and compared results against known mining operations as an accuracy check.

Vog Exposure and Respiratory Vulnerability in Hawaiʻi

Course: GIS4424C Applications in GIS for Disease Ecology and Zoonoses Date: Fall 2025
  • Applied spatial-statistics workflows (KDE, Global Moran’s I, Local Moran’s I/LISA, Getis-Ord Gi*) to detect clusters and hot/cold spots in a tract-level risk surface.
  • Built a tract-level “Vog Risk Index” by combining modeled exposure with asthma and COPD prevalence using z-scores.
  • Integrated air-quality monitoring data (SO₂, PM₂.₅) with health-surveillance data and 2020 census-tract polygons for spatial analysis.

Structural and stratigraphic relationships across the northern boundary of the high-pressure terrane in the Brooks Range orogen (northern Alaska)

Course: Undergraduate Research (Honors Thesis) Date: Spring 2026
  • Defined research questions about fault attitude/type, structural relationships between mapped fault systems, and stratigraphic age relationships across fault-bounded areas.
  • Proposed a workflow using mapping + U–Pb detrital zircon geochronology to constrain stratigraphy and protolith ages (maximum depositional ages and age-peak comparisons).
  • Connected structural + geochronologic results to test tectonic development/exhumation models for the Brooks Range.
