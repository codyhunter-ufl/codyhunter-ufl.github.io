Increasing Sea Surface Temperature and its Effect on the Florida Keys' Reefs

Course: GIS3043 — Foundations of GIS
Date: Spring 2024

Overview

This project examined the spatial relationship between rising sea surface temperatures (SST) and coral decline along the Florida Keys reef system, integrating long-term monitoring data across three study years to evaluate temporal change in both thermal conditions and hard coral counts.

Achieved Goals

  • Applied Inverse Distance Weighted (IDW) interpolation in ArcGIS Pro to sea surface temperature and hard coral count data across three time periods (2009, 2015, 2022), using a modified power value (B²=0.5) for coral counts to reduce the influence of spatial outliers.
  • Summarized CREMP monitoring station data using Microsoft Excel pivot tables and joined results to ArcGIS Pro station shapefiles to spatially link average SST and total hard coral counts per station for each study year.
  • Compared interpolated SST and hard coral density surfaces across the three study years to evaluate temporal change and assess the spatial relationship between rising temperatures and coral decline along the Florida Keys reef system.
